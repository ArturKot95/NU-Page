What is the Neisse University?

Neisse University is an international “Information and Communication Management” bachelor’s degree study program in English language within three countries of the Neisse Region. This program provides education in the fields of economy, management, marketing, psychology, communication and IT. The studies begin in the Czech Republic, continue in Poland, and are accomplished in Germany. Furthermore, the program includes a 3-month internship anywhere in the world.

The goal of the studies is not only to gain professional skills but to get experience and personal growth during the education process. As a student of the Neisse University, you will get an opportunity to gain working process experience in multicultural groups, to understand principles of team building, and to find out about the benefits of studying in an international environment.

Neisse program accepts students from all over the world – Europe, Africa, Asia as well as North or South America.

About the Neisse University

Since 2001 the Neisse University runs only an “Information and communication management” branch. The program's main focus is on management and computer science; however, it includes economics, psychology, communication as well as rhetoric, with the goal to provide a balanced combination of technical and social skills.

Education Program

The B.Sc. in Information and Communication Management is a six-semester full-time program. Students are required to take all the modules during each semester as is indicated below. According to the European Credit Transfer System (ECTS) students will acquire 180 credit points and qualify for Master's programs.

Czech Republic

The first term education program is focused on creating foundations of informatics and economy with basics of psychology. During second term there is much more focus on economy, marketing, and psychology, as well as continuous acquirement of better basic IT knowledge. There is also one week of an excellent course of Argumentation and Rhetoric with the famous British professor Steve Wheeler, who is already cooperating with the Neisse University for many years.

Poland

Studies in Poland are in particular related to IT, accounting, communication, and law. Students build up on basics they’ve acquired in the Czech Republic. Furthermore, there is one week course, this time about European law.

Germany

During the fifth semester, you will get deeply into information and communication science. Students organize the annual Neisse Meeting as a part of their studies for all current students and graduates. The last semester is composed of an internship and writing of the bachelor's thesis.

Traineeship all over the World

One great advantage of this study program is a 3-month internship in any country around the world. That will give you an excellent opportunity to try yourself in an international company and to learn from experienced professionals. Students of the Neisse University had completed practice terms in more than 20 countries – including Japan and the USA – in the following companies: IBM, Google, Skoda, and such.

Visa to the Czech Republic

After the application to the Hochschule Zittau/Gorlitz, you will receive a letter of admission, but to start studies at the Neisse University you have to get Czech visa. Applicants need to go to their respective Czech Embassy or Consulate to apply for the visa. One of the requirements is a letter of admission from the Czech university. To get that document you have to make a request to the Neisse department in Liberec. Please apply for the letter of admission from the TUL with your received letter of admission from Hochschule Zittau/Gorlitz (via e-mail).

Coordinator contact in the Czech Republic:

Mgr. Hana Starova
Tel.: +420 48535 2501
E-mail: hana.starova@tul.cz

Application for a student visa to the Czech Republic is considered by the Czech Embassy from 60 days to six months, it is advised to take this fact into account and prepare for a visa in advance. Apply at the embassy for a long-term student visa to the Czech Republic called "long-term visa for purpose of learning.", it is possible only through the internet page www.visapoint.eu.

When applying through the Visapoint system there can occur a problem that more people apply for an appointment then is an amount of available places. The system may issue a message that there is no space available and the applicant should try to register later. Therefore, the registration process may take several days or even weeks, depending on the city, the embassies or the time of year.

Application for a student visa to the Czech Republic must be provided in the consulate in person. All documents must be translated into the Czech language by the court interpreter. All printing copies must be certified by a Czech notary. Any documents older than 180 days will not be accepted. The passport must be valid for at least another 90 days after the expected expiry of the visa.

Application form and instructions for filling can be found on the website of the Czech Embassy of your country.

Generally, it is necessary to provide following documents for a visa:

  • Passport and 2 copies of the passport page with a photo, a minimum period of validity of the passport has to be 11 months from the date of filing.
  • 2 photos, the size of 3.5 by 4.5 cm. On the reverse side of the photo must sign in Latin letters in accordance with the passport (name, surname, date of birth).
  • Confirmation of acceptance to study (your letter of admission)
  • Proof of financial support. To obtain a long-term student visa to the Czech Republic for a period of six months is necessary to have a sum of at least 59.400 CZK for six months or 85.800 CZK for the year.
  • Czech accommodation proving (contact the Neisse Department in Liberec to get more information).
  • Czech health insurance proving (you can have any private Czech insurance, which will cover the insurance cost which is requested by Czech embassy in your country (usually 30.000 euro.)

When applying for a long-term student visa to the Czech Republic you may be interviewed by the Czech officer. At the interview, you may be asked where you will study, how to accurately called the school and where it is, where you will live, what are the conditions in the place of your future residence in the Czech Republic. If you were not in the Czech Republic yet and have not seen a university or a dormitory, it is desirable to say that you have seen the pictures and describe what you see on them. You can also be asked what for actually you need a student visa to the Czech Republic, why do you need to study in the Czech Republic and what are you going to do after graduation. Please prepare yourself for that interview.

We strongly advise applicants to apply as early as possible for the visa in their home countries as the entire process is enormously time-consuming.

Costs

This education program is free of charge for European Union citizens. For non-EU students, the tuition cost is approximately 1500 Euro per semester, except for the last two semesters in Germany that are free of charge for everyone.

Czech Republic

In the Czech Republic, the rental payment is approximately 2500-3500 CZK (90-130 EUR) per month. The final cost depends on the type of the room, and the number of roommates. Food and clothing costs are approximately 4000 CZK (150 EUR). The payments for leisure time are also very individual. If you wish to go out to clubs and restaurants with your friends, expect to spend at least 1000 CZK (40 EUR) or more. The university fee (for registration, student card, etc.) in the Czech Republic is approximately 300 CZK (11 EUR).

Poland

In Poland, the cost of living is a bit lower than in the Czech Republic. For the student accommodation, it is around 300-350 PLN (70-80 EUR) and a deposit of 200 PLN (50 EUR). The food and clothing spending is very similar to the Czech Republic. The university fee in Poland is around 30 PLN (7 EUR).

Germany

In Germany, living costs are the highest. For accommodation, you will be required to pay approximately 190-270 EUR. For the food and necessities, you have to expect spending around 200 EUR. For the fun and some parties, you can add another 50 EUR. The university fee in Germany is around 160 EUR in total. You will pay approximately 80 EUR for a semester.

Continuous Education and Job Opportunities

Most of the graduates continue with master studies – they often choose another study in English in a foreign country and choose majors such as international management, economics or IT. Neisse programs give a perfect opportunity to try yourself in (the) different areas and to choose the best master program to continue with your education. Graduates can start their professional career in key areas of international business administration like marketing, corporate finance, accounting, management. Also, the fields of economy, computer science or even running your own business are common career paths after the graduation.
